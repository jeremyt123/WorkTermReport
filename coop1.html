Job Description

Each day working at Blueprint brings new challenges and technologies but its always a great learning experience

Since I work on so many different projects day to day I will go over my 3 favorite projects.

Modern Sales Co-op

Modern Sales Co-op is a company that connects store owners to product vendors all across Canada. Although looking very simple on the front-end, Modern Sales has an extremely large member/staff portal that is responsible for a large amount of their business operation

Due to the site becoming outdated I was tasked with rebuilding the site and all functionality with assistance from our senior developer

SOME parts of the member portal include:

  • Extranet
    Extranet is where most of their users will be. Extranet is a one-stop-shop for members to get any information they need about vendors. Extranet has a Forum where users can post and ask questions, A News section where Admins can post product/shipment updates that specific users can see depending on role, Vendor Black Book, New Product Releases, and many other features. Extranet receives a lot of traffic and usually sees around 500-700k visits per year.

  • EATS - Estimated Arrival Time System
    The EATS system is responsible for keeping track of all shipment times and provides accurate shipping time estimates based off location, product, and quantity. The development of EATS required massive SQL tables and huge queries to link all the incoming data together.

  • Admin Dashboard
    Although only used by a small subset of users the admin panel has a ton of functionality. In the admin panel you can find a User Role management system, Control over all vendors and members, Event planning, management, inviting, and ticket sales, and site traffic statistics to name a few.

Technologies used: PHP, mySQL, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, jQuery, Concrete5, cPanel, phpMyAdmin, and Various PHP Libraries

A Future That Fits - Halton District School Board (HDSB)

Due to the pandemic, HDSB was not able to hold their annual Specialist High Skills Major (SHSM) Information event in person. A Future That Fits was the replacement for that event.

On the day of the event students could enter the site, choose a program that they were interested in, see what schools offered that program, and join a google meet with the schools program supervisor to get information and ask questions about the program.

The challenge with this site was being able to handle the traffic on the day of the event as we were expecting 4000 students to join at around the same time causing a huge load on our server.

This site was completely my project and I built every part of it. Using CloudFlare, AWS, and Loader.io I was able to setup the site so that on the day of we were able to handle 5000 unique visitors (turns out parents were also interested in attending).

Technologies used: CloudFlare, AWS, Loader.io, PHP, HTML, LESS, JavaScript, jQuery, Concrete5, Adobe XD, cPanel, Google Analytics

Pico Tradeshow

The Pico Tradeshow site was another example of a company continuing their previously in-person business during a pandemic.

Pico sells alot of products and in order to tell their customers about them they have in-person tradeshows throughout the year.

This site was their way to continue to have these tradeshows virutually. During a tradeshow customers can join this site and join one of the 4 possible Zoom calls displayed on the home page.

Customers can watch the zoom calls to see the new products Pico have to offer. If a customer doesn't feel like watching the zoom call they can also view the catalogue or the featured tradeshow products.

Customers can even order the tradeshow products straight off the site at any point during the tradeshow.

My contribution to this site was developing the shopping cart functionality, assisting in developing the wordpress plugin for Pico to setup these tradeshows, developing the featured products section, and a few other bits and pieces

Technologies used: PHP, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, jQuery, Wordpress, Adobe XD, cPanel, phpMyAdmin
